What to look for in an AI newsletter

After comparing these, three things stand out as the real differentiators:

1. Curation method matters more than source count. Most newsletters pull from the same places — arXiv, Hacker News, Twitter. The difference is how they filter. Human editors bring judgment but are slow and can only process so much. AI can read everything but needs good prompts to surface what's genuinely important, not just what's getting clicks.

2. Weekly is too slow for AI. In 2026, the AI landscape shifts significantly every few days. A weekly newsletter means you're always a week behind. Daily is the minimum viable frequency if you actually want to stay current — but it has to be short enough that you'll actually read it.

3. Recurring subscriptions add up fast. $10/month doesn't sound like much until you realize you've paid $120/year for something you skim in 3 minutes. The best value in this space right now is a one-time payment model — you pay once and you're done. No renewal anxiety, no "am I getting enough value to justify the subscription" math.
