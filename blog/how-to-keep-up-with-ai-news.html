I was chatting with a friend the other day, and one topic just kept circling back — AI anxiety. It seems like every group chat eventually lands there now. More and more people around me have started saying things that would have sounded paranoid a year ago:

"AI is making us dumber."
"AI is coming for my job."
"Learn AI or get left behind."

I'm sure you've heard some version of this too. Maybe you've even said it yourself.

There's no denying it — artificial intelligence is moving at a genuinely astonishing pace right now. New models drop every other week. Papers flood arXiv daily. Every social feed is a firehose of demos, takes, and predictions. It can feel like standing in front of a wave you can't possibly outrun.

But here's what I actually believe: we're not at the panic stage yet. Not even close.

Anxiety lives in the unknown

Think about what most AI anxiety actually is. It's not fear of a specific model or a particular paper. It's the vague, gnawing sense that something big is happening and you're not sure what it means — for your career, your skills, your relevance.

That feeling doesn't come from knowing too much. It comes from knowing too little.

When you actually start following what's happening — reading the papers, tracking the tools, understanding the trends — something shifts. The monster under the bed turns out to be a machine learning model. An impressive one, sure. But understandable. Knowable.

The problem, of course, is time. Nobody wants to burn their precious free time wading through an ocean of information. You've got a life. You've got work. You don't need a second job keeping up with AI.
